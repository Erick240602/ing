Korea.

.:

We will visit Seoul, which has been the capital of Korea for 600 years. It is divided into two parts due to the course of the Hangang River: in the northern part all the history and culture is concentrated, while the southern part is known as the commercial area. This creates a contrast where the skyscrapers stand out over the Buddhist temples. Afterwards, meet your guide and head out for a full day of exploring the South Korean capital at Jihachul, the local subway. We start the day by visiting Gyeongbokgung Palace, this "Brilliant Palace of Happiness" is one of the five palaces of the Choson (Joseon) dynasty. The visit includes the Folklore Museum and its collection of objects from traditional Korean life. Next, we will visit the Jogyesa Temple, one of the largest in the city and the center of the Jogyejong Buddhist order, which promotes Zen meditation. Lunch at a local restaurant where we will taste a delicious Bibimbap (bowl of rice with vegetables and meat), we will walk through the Bukchon district, this district, nicknamed the "Northern Village", is home to about 900 traditional Korean houses and promises a unique cultural immersion in ancient Korea. Next we will take a tour of Insadong Street, which is full of antique shops and art galleries. We will end with an incredible entertainment show on stage "Cooking Nanta", this non-verbal comedy, animated to the rhythm of kitchen utensils, combines traditional music, much more modern and intelligently mixes the genres of comedy and drama.

Departure with the guide to the KTX train that will take us to Gyeongju, the spiritual and cultural capital of Korea. We will take a tour of the city, what is known as the “Museum without walls”. This ancient capital of the Shilla Kingdom was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2000. We will visit the Bulkugsa Temple, then tour the mound park dotted with the 23 tombs of the rulers of the Shilla Kingdom and their families and visit the tomb of the "Celestial Horse". We will continue visiting the tombs of Noseo-dong and make a final stop at the oldest observatory in the Far East, the Cheomseongdae tower built in the year 634, where royal astronomers studied the movements of the stars and predicted eclipses of the sun and moon. We will visit the cultural village of

.:

. Gamcheon, an old neighborhood located on a hill. Here, houses were built by people fleeing war, the last enclave during the Korean War. Today, it is an artistic town decorated with murals and sculptures created by residents. We will make a stop at the United Nations Memorial Cemetery, the only one in the world, in fact, it has been managed by the United Nations since 1960. This place pays tribute to the 37,895 members of the UN forces who died in the War of Korea between 1950 and 1953. Lunch at a local restaurant.
